# Profile Templates

Use these as drafting patterns. Adapt to the user's market, voice, seniority, and proof.

## Positioning Statement

Format:

`I help [audience] achieve [outcome] through [method/expertise], backed by [proof].`

Examples:

- `I help B2B SaaS teams turn messy customer workflows into AI-assisted internal tools, backed by shipped production apps and measurable cycle-time reductions.`
- `I build data and automation systems for operations teams that need fewer manual handoffs and clearer reporting.`

## Headline Formulas

Recruiter-facing:

`[Target Role] | [Domain/Industry] | [Top Skills/Tools] | [Proof or Specialty]`

Consultant/client-facing:

`I help [ICP] [business outcome] with [method] | [Proof] | [Keywords]`

Founder/executive:

`[Role] at [Company] | Building [category/outcome] for [audience] | [Credibility keywords]`

Technical expert:

`[Specialty] Engineer | [Stack/Domain] | Shipping [type of systems] for [business/user outcome]`

Rules:

- Put the strongest searchable identity early.
- Include 3 to 6 high-value keywords if truthful.
- Avoid generic "open to opportunities" unless it is part of a deliberate job-search signal.

## About Section

Recommended structure:

1. First 2 lines: clear identity and outcome.
2. Proof: strongest measurable achievements, shipped work, clients, roles, or projects.
3. Expertise: domains, tools, methods, industries, and problems solved.
4. Current direction: what the user is looking for or building now.
5. Call to action: why someone should connect, message, hire, refer, or follow.

About skeleton:

```text
I [do/build/lead/help] [audience] [outcome] through [expertise/method].

Recent proof:
- [Metric or shipped project]
- [Metric or responsibility scope]
- [Credible artifact, role, client, certification, or domain depth]

My strongest lanes are [keyword cluster 1], [keyword cluster 2], and [keyword cluster 3]. I am especially interested in [target problems/opportunities].

If you are working on [specific opportunity], message me about [specific next action].
```

## Experience Bullets

Use this pattern:

`[Action verb] [thing built/led/improved] for [audience/system], resulting in [measurable outcome], using [tools/methods].`

If metrics are unavailable:

`[Action verb] [scope] across [team/system/customer], improving [quality/speed/risk/clarity] by [observable evidence].`

Strong verbs:

- Built, launched, led, designed, automated, scaled, reduced, improved, migrated, analyzed, shipped, negotiated, partnered, implemented, trained, secured, optimized

Weak verbs to replace:

- Responsible for, helped with, worked on, assisted, participated in, passionate about

## Featured Section Strategy

Use 3 to 5 proof assets:

- Case study or portfolio page
- Demo video or product walkthrough
- High-signal LinkedIn post or newsletter
- Resume or one-page capability brief
- GitHub/project link, deck, talk, article, media feature, certification, or testimonial

Each asset should answer one question:

- Can this person do the work?
- Have they done it before?
- Do they think clearly?
- Are they credible enough to message?

## Skills Strategy

Cluster skills into:

- Role identity: product manager, software engineer, data analyst, founder, account executive
- Domain: AI, fintech, healthcare, B2B SaaS, growth, cybersecurity, supply chain
- Tools: Python, React, SQL, Salesforce, HubSpot, Figma, AWS, Azure, Tableau
- Methods: experimentation, prompt engineering, user research, ETL, lifecycle marketing, GTM strategy
- Outcomes: automation, revenue growth, conversion optimization, risk reduction, cost savings

Prioritize the top visible skills for the target audience. Add supporting skills only when they are truthful and strategically relevant.

## Recommendation Request

```text
Hi [Name], I am updating my LinkedIn around [target role/offer]. Would you be open to writing a short recommendation about our work on [project]?

The most helpful angle would be [specific credibility: execution speed, technical quality, leadership, communication, business impact, client trust]. A few concrete details about [result/scope] would help a lot.

Happy to draft a few memory joggers if useful.
```

## Content Pillars

Choose 3 to 5:

- Proof of work: breakdowns, demos, case studies, before/after
- Teaching: practical lessons, frameworks, checklists, teardown posts
- Point of view: where the industry is wrong, what good looks like, tradeoffs
- Founder/operator notes: decisions, constraints, lessons learned
- Market intelligence: role/client trends, tool changes, hiring insights
- Personal credibility: origin story, values, working style, selective wins

Post formats:

- `Problem -> why it matters -> lesson -> example -> takeaway`
- `Before -> intervention -> after -> what I would do differently`
- `Myth -> reality -> implication -> practical advice`
- `List of mistakes -> fix for each -> one closing principle`
