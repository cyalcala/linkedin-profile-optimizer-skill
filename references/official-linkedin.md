## Durable Takeaways

- A complete profile is a credibility and discoverability asset. Photo, headline, About, experience, skills, recommendations, and custom URL all matter.
- Recruiter discovery depends heavily on searchable terms: titles, skills, tools, industries, geography, seniority, and Boolean-style combinations.
- LinkedIn skills are part of the matching and classification layer. As of the refresh date, LinkedIn Help says profiles can list up to 100 skills in the Skills section.
- Featured content should support proof: portfolio links, case studies, media, posts, newsletters, documents, product demos, or credible artifacts.
- Recommendations should be specific. The best requests ask a credible person to vouch for a specific skill, project, leadership trait, or outcome.
- Content should provide professional knowledge or advice. Empty virality tactics, engagement pods, fake comments, and vague inspiration are fragile and can harm trust.
- Verification and skill validation are growing trust signals. Use them when available, but never imply verification or credentials the user does not have.
