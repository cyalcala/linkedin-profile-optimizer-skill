# LinkedIn Audit Scorecard

Score each category from 0 to 5.

0 means missing or harmful. 3 means acceptable but generic. 5 means specific, credible, and aligned with the target audience.

## Categories

1. Positioning clarity
   - The profile makes it obvious who the person is, who they help, and what opportunity they want.

2. Search relevance
   - Target titles, skills, tools, industries, and seniority signals appear naturally in high-value sections.

3. Proof density
   - Claims are backed by metrics, shipped work, case studies, recognized employers, clients, credentials, testimonials, or artifacts.

4. Trust surface
   - Photo, banner, verification, recommendations, Featured assets, activity, and links make the profile feel real and credible.

5. About section strength
   - The first lines are compelling, the story is focused, and the reader sees evidence plus next action.

6. Experience section strength
   - Experience bullets emphasize outcomes, scope, tools, and business/user impact rather than duties.

7. Skills architecture
   - Top skills align with the target and are supported by experience, projects, certifications, or content.

8. Conversion path
   - Recruiters, clients, investors, or collaborators know what to do next.

9. Content alignment
   - Recent activity reinforces the positioning instead of diluting it.

10. Authenticity and risk
   - The profile avoids inflated claims, spammy tactics, fake engagement, confidentiality leaks, or keyword stuffing.

## Interpretation

- 45 to 50: strong profile. Optimize small proof and conversion details.
- 35 to 44: credible profile with important missed upside.
- 25 to 34: profile likely undersells the person or misses search demand.
- Under 25: rebuild positioning, proof, and section architecture.

## Acceptance Criteria

A rebuilt profile is ready when:

- The target audience can classify the person within 5 seconds.
- The top keyword cluster appears in multiple relevant sections without feeling stuffed.
- Every major claim has proof or is softened into an honest statement.
- The Featured section gives fast evidence.
- The profile contains a next step for the intended audience.
